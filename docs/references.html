Thus, biradicals are most easily understood as two doublets in a molecule. In other words, the electron exchange interaction between the two unpaired electrons is negligible or nearly negligible for biradicals because of the long distance between the two electrons. When the magnitude of the dipole−dipole interaction in a molecule is large enough to produce two spin states, namely, singlet and triplet, the molecular species with the two unpaired electrons is referred to as a diradical.
